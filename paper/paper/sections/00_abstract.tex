\begin{abstract}
Visual-language tracking aims to localize a target object by jointly exploiting visual observations and natural language descriptions. Existing trackers often use language as an auxiliary input but provide limited explicit supervision on whether visual representations are semantically aligned with textual cues. In this paper, we propose SALTTrack, a semantic alignment framework for robust visual-language tracking. The core idea is to regularize visual representations with text-guided semantic alignment, encouraging prediction features to move toward the target semantics while avoiding representation collapse. We further introduce a lightweight routed LoRA tuning strategy to adapt the tracker efficiently with a small number of trainable parameters. Extensive experiments on representative visual-language tracking benchmarks demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed method.
\end{abstract}
